### Concept 1 — "Onye wetara oji"

**Concept name:** Kola Nut Ceremony / コラの実の儀式

**Visual description:**
A single kola nut (oji) drawn in the style of a Japanese woodblock print (ukiyo-e). The nut sits centered on a cream/aged-paper background. Thin ink lines define the lobes of the kola nut; a faint circular red hanko (stamp seal) sits in the lower-right corner with the kanji 「感謝」(gratitude). Small decorative sakura petals drift around the kola nut — blending the ceremony of the Igbo kola presentation with the Japanese mono no aware (transience) aesthetic. Subtle indigo-blue border in a traditional Japanese shippo (interlocking circles) pattern frames the whole composition.

**Text/typography:**
- Main proverb in tategaki (vertical right-to-left Japanese script): 「コラの実を持ってくる者が命を持ってくる」
- Romanized Igbo subtitle in small serif font below: *Onye wetara oji wetara ndụ*
- English micro-text at bottom edge: "He who brings kola brings life — Igbo proverb"
- Font style: Main text in a brushstroke (fude) style font (e.g. "Hina Mincho" or hand-lettered brush). Subtitle in clean italic serif.

**Products:** T-shirt, tote bag, mug, sticker

**Target buyer:** Nigerian diaspora in Japan; Japanese people curious about African culture; Igbo cultural pride buyers globally

**Suzuri category:** Tシャツ (T-shirts), マグカップ (Mugs), トートバッグ (Tote bags)

**Redbubble tags:** `igbo proverb`, `nigerian japan`, `afro japanese`, `kola nut art`, `african diaspora`

### Concept 2 — "Egbe bere, ugo bere"

**Concept name:** Kite and Eagle Balance / 共存の鷹

**Visual description:**
Two birds in flight — a kite (egbe) on the left and an eagle (ugo) on the right — rendered in minimalist Japanese sumi-e (ink wash) painting style. The birds face each other symmetrically, wings spread, tips nearly touching at center. Background is pure white with a single horizontal brushstroke of pale blue-grey. The composition mirrors Japanese enso circle philosophy — the idea of completeness and balance. Below each bird, a small flag icon: Nigerian green-white-green and Japanese red-white hinomaru. The birds' feathers use subtle ankara-inspired geometric patterns in terracotta and indigo, visible only on close inspection.

**Text/typography:**
- Tategaki main text: 「鷹が安全に降りてくれば、鷲も安全に降りる」
- Igbo romanization: *Egbe bere, ugo bere — nke si ibe ya ebela, nku kwa ya*
- English below: "Let the kite perch, let the eagle perch — live and let live"
- Font: Tategaki in Noto Serif JP (elegant, traditional); English in small condensed sans-serif

**Products:** T-shirt, phone case, tote bag, poster print, sticker

**Target buyer:** Multicultural identity buyers; Nigerian professionals in Japan; peace/coexistence message buyers globally

**Suzuri category:** Tシャツ, スマホケース, ステッカー

**Redbubble tags:** `igbo proverb`, `live and let live`, `afro japanese fusion`, `nigerian culture`, `sumi-e bird`

### Concept 3 — "Aka dike"

**Concept name:** Strong Arm / 力の腕

**Visual description:**
A bold, graphic arm raised in a fist — drawn in the style of vintage Japanese tatami-room art with strong black outlines and flat color fills. The arm has ankara-pattern fabric as the sleeve — diamond and triangle motifs in red, yellow, and black. The fist itself is rendered in warm brown skin tones. Around the arm, four Japanese kanji characters are arranged in a square: 「力」「勇」「知」「心」(strength, courage, wisdom, heart). The whole design sits on a forest-green background. A small rising sun motif (Japanese) merges into a Nigerian sunburst in the upper corner.

**Text/typography:**
- Centered below fist in tategaki: 「強者の手は隠れた力を持つ」
- Igbo romanization: *Aka dike anaghị ama n'azu*
- English: "The arm of the brave has unseen power"
- Font: Bold, blocky Japanese gothic (Gothic MB101 style); Igbo in clean sans-serif caps

**Products:** T-shirt, hoodie, sticker, phone case

**Target buyer:** Afrocentric empowerment buyers; Nigerian athletes/fitness community in Japan; pan-African diaspora

**Suzuri category:** Tシャツ, パーカー, スマホケース

### Concept 8 — "Bowing 90 Degrees"

**Concept name:** The Deep Bow / 90度のお辞儀

**Visual description:**
The Naija Salaryman character performing a perfect 90-degree Japanese bow — a sign of deepest respect — to an elderly Nigerian grandmother (iyom figure) in full wrapper and headgear (gele-style), who is also bowing back in the Igbo style (one knee slightly bent, hands respectfully positioned). They are bowing to each other in front of a split background: left half is a Japanese corporate building entrance; right half is a Nigerian compound with a beautiful painted exterior wall. The image is warm and touching — two cultures' deepest forms of respect meeting in the middle.

**Text/typography:**
- Tategaki caption: 「敬意はすべての文化に共通する」(Respect is universal across all cultures)
- Bottom bilingual text: "どうぞ / Biko, before you nah" (Japanese polite "after you" + Igbo/Nigerian Pidgin)

### Concept 12 — "Ankara Kimono"

**Concept name:** Kimono in Ankara / アンカラの着物

**Visual description:**
A flat fashion illustration of a kimono silhouette — the classic wide-sleeved, floor-length Japanese kimono shape — but the fabric pattern is entirely composed of West African ankara wax print motifs. The specific ankara pattern uses concentric diamond shapes (a common Igbo-region motif) in deep indigo, burnt orange, and cream. The obi (kimono sash) uses a contrasting ankara pattern with smaller geometric repeats. The background is a soft gradient from deep midnight blue (top) to terracotta (bottom). The figure wears no face — it is a design/fashion illustration, not a portrait — giving it a timeless quality. The composition is elegant and symmetrical.

**Text/typography:**
- Minimal text: Simply 「アンカラ着物」(Ankara Kimono) in elegant tategaki, bottom right
- Optional: Small maker's seal 「Naija × Nippon」in a circular logo form
- Font: Thin, elegant Mincho (Ming) style tategaki; no heavy text elements — let the visual speak

**Products:** T-shirt, tote bag, phone case, wall art poster, hoodie

**Concept name:** Stitched Together / 縫い合わせた文化

**Visual description:**
A single square design composed entirely of intricate geometric patterns — left side in traditional Japanese sashiko embroidery patterns (white stitch lines on indigo navy: hemp leaf, seven treasures, shippo), right side in traditional Igbo/Ikwere geometric patterns (similar bold repeat geometry in terracotta stitching on deep green). The two halves interlock in the center — the lines of one pattern continue into and blend with the other, creating a seamless fusion zone at the center where both pattern systems coexist and overlap. The effect is meditative and beautiful. No photography — pure geometric illustration.

### Concept 15 — "Nye Nda" (Give Thanks)

**Concept name:** Ikwere Gratitude / イクウェレの感謝

**Visual description:**
A flowing calligraphy-style design. The Ikwere phrase "NYE NDA" (give thanks / thank you) written large in a style that mimics Japanese shodo (brush calligraphy) — thick and thin strokes, ink wash texture — but the letterforms are clearly Roman script, making it bilingual in aesthetic. Below and to the right, the Japanese equivalent: 「ありがとう」in actual shodo style. Both phrases are surrounded by uli-style Ikwere decorative marks — flowing curved lines and dots that echo the decorative body-painting tradition of Rivers State women. The background is soft — cream or aged paper texture. A single red circle (like a Japanese seal) sits in the bottom right corner with the text "Naija × Nippon" inside.

**Text/typography:**
- Large calligraphic: "NYE NDA" (Ikwere — Give thanks)
- Shodo below: 「ありがとう」
- Small bilingual: "Give thanks — in every language"
- Red hanko seal: "Naija × Nippon"
- Font: Both phrases in brush calligraphy style — hand-lettered appearance; body copy in clean sans-serif

**Products:** T-shirt, greeting card/postcard print, mug, sticker, tote bag

**Target buyer:** Ikwere language pride buyers; gratitude/positivity buyers; Japanese buyers who appreciate calligraphy-style art; diaspora gift buyers

**Suzuri category:** Tシャツ, マグカップ, トートバッグ, ステッカー
