**Business:** Naija x Nippon Merch (`naija-in-japan-merch`)

## Week 1 (Days 1–7): Foundation Setup

**Goal:** Accounts live, first 5 designs production-ready, zero revenue expected yet.

### Day 1 — Account Creation
- [ ] Create Suzuri account at suzuri.jp — use operator email, set shop name: **ナイジャ×ニッポン (Naija x Nippon)**
  - Suzuri shop URL will be: `suzuri.jp/naija_x_nippon`
  - Upload profile photo: simple "N×N" monogram logo on emerald green background
  - Write shop description in Japanese: 「ナイジェリア文化と日本の美学が出会うブランドです。イボ語のことわざ、ラゴス×東京のデザイン、アフロ・ジャパニーズフュージョンアート。」
- [ ] Create Redbubble account at redbubble.com — shop name: **NaijaxNippon**
  - Redbubble shop URL: `redbubble.com/people/NaijaxNippon`
  - Same monogram profile photo
  - Bio: "Afro-Japanese fusion merch. Nigerian culture meets Japanese aesthetics. Igbo proverbs in Japanese script, Lagos × Tokyo mashups, Ikwere language pride. Made by a Nigerian living in Japan."
- [ ] Create Instagram account: **@naijaXnippon**
  - Bio: 🇳🇬×🇯🇵 Afro-Japanese merch | Igbo proverbs in Japanese script | Lagos × Tokyo | Shop: link in bio
  - Add Linktree or Beacons link pointing to both Suzuri + Redbubble shops
- [ ] Create Pinterest business account: **Naija x Nippon**

### Days 2–3 — Design Production: First 5 Designs
Produce designs using Canva Pro or Midjourney + Canva. Priority designs for first upload:

| # | Concept | Why First |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Kola Nut Ceremony (Concept 1) | Strong cultural anchor, evergreen |
| 6 | Agbada Salaryman (Concept 6) | High humor/shareability, most viral potential |
| 9 | Danfo × Densha (Concept 9) | Transit fans + urban culture — wide appeal |
| 10 | Jollof Bento (Concept 10) | Food content = high engagement on Instagram |
| 14 | Ikwere na Tokyo (Concept 14) | Unique niche — zero competition |

**Production checklist per design:**
- [ ] Main artwork: 4500x4500px PNG, 300dpi, transparent background (for Redbubble)
- [ ] Suzuri variant: 3000x3000px, RGB, white background optional for dark-garment designs
- [ ] Mockup for Instagram: use Suzuri's automatic mockup or Placeit.net

### Days 4–5 — Suzuri Upload (5 designs)
- Upload all 5 designs to Suzuri
- For each product, enable: Tシャツ, マグカップ, トートバッグ, スマホケース, ステッカー
- Set pricing: Suzuri sets base price; add ¥300–500 profit margin for T-shirts, ¥200 for stickers
- Write Japanese product descriptions using keywords: アフロジャパニーズ, ナイジェリア, イボ語, ラゴス東京, アフリカン

### Days 6–7 — Redbubble Upload (5 designs)
- Upload same 5 designs to Redbubble
- Enable all relevant product types (stickers, T-shirts, mugs, tote bags, phone cases, prints)
- Set markup: 20% above base price (Redbubble standard competitive range)
- Apply 5 specific tags per design (see design-concepts.md) + 3 general tags: `afro japanese`, `nigeria japan`, `multicultural art`
- Fill all SEO fields: title, description, tags

## Week 4 (Days 22–30): Community Outreach — African Community in Tokyo

**Goal:** First human connections, first shares, potential first sales from warm audience.

### Day 22 — Research Tokyo African Community Groups
Find and list:
- Facebook groups: "Nigerians in Japan", "African Community Tokyo", "African Diaspora Japan"
- Instagram accounts of Nigerian/African community organizations in Tokyo
- LINE groups for African expats in Japan (ask in Facebook groups)
- Physical locations: African restaurants, barbershops, churches with Nigerian congregation in Tokyo, Yokohama, Osaka

Key churches/communities to research:
- RCCG (Redeemed Christian Church of God) Tokyo
- Mountain of Fire Tokyo
- African community at Okubo (Tokyo's "Little Asia" neighborhood)

### Days 23–25 — Outreach Messages
Send personalized direct messages (not spam) to:
- 10 Nigerian expat Instagram accounts with 500–5000 followers (micro-influencers)
- 5 African community organization accounts

### Days 26–27 — Community Posts
Post in relevant Facebook groups (where allowed):

### Days 28–30 — Review, Optimize, Plan Month 2
