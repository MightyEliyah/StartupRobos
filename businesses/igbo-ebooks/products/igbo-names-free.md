## Why Igbo Names Matter

A name is not decoration — in Igbo culture, a name is a prayer spoken at birth, a story passed across generations, and a thread that keeps diaspora children connected to the land of their ancestors even when they grow up thousands of miles away. Whether you are a parent in Tokyo, London, Houston, or Osaka, choosing an Igbo name for your child is one of the most powerful acts of cultural preservation you will ever make.

## How to Use This Guide

Each entry includes:
- **Name** — written in standard Igbo orthography
- **Meaning** — literal + cultural meaning
- **Gender** — M (Male), F (Female), U (Unisex)
- **Usage Note** — context, region, or spiritual significance

## A Note for Diaspora Parents

Choosing an Igbo name for your child is not nostalgia — it is strategy. A name roots a child in identity before they can speak. It gives grandparents a way to speak to them in their language. It makes elders in the village smile. And it reminds the child, every day of their life, that they came from somewhere meaningful.

If you are raising children outside Nigeria, this name is one of the most durable gifts you can give.
